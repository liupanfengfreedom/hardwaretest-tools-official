Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is my camera not displaying properly?

This may be due to browser permission restrictions or camera hardware issues. Please ensure: 1) Allow browser access to camera; 2) Check camera connection; 3) Ensure no other programs are using the camera.

How to test camera frame rate?

Check the real-time FPS value at the top right of the page. Professional cameras typically support 30fps or higher, high-end cameras can reach 60fps or more. Stable frame rate is key to smooth video.

Learn more: FPS Frame Rate Special Test

How to determine camera quality issues?

If the camera: 1) Video is blurry; 2) Frame rate is too low causing lag; 3) Color distortion; 4) Poor performance in low light, may indicate camera quality issues.

Troubleshooting: Camera Troubleshooting Guide

What camera types are supported?

Supports all camera types: laptop built-in cameras, USB external cameras, network cameras, conference cameras, streaming cameras, etc. Compatible with Windows, macOS, Linux systems.

How to test camera resolution?

The tool automatically displays current resolution. High-quality cameras typically support 1080p(1920x1080) or higher, ordinary cameras may only support 720p(1280x720).

Special test: Camera Resolution Special Test

Is test data saved?

All test data is only saved in the current browser session. Data will be lost after refreshing the page or closing the tab. We do not collect or upload any camera data, ensuring your privacy and security.

Data Security: All data processed locally only
