Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is my camera not displaying?

This may be due to browser permission restrictions or camera hardware issues. Please ensure: 1) You allow the browser to access the camera; 2) The camera is properly connected; 3) No other application is currently using the camera.

How do I test camera frame rate?

Look at the real-time FPS value in the top right corner of the page. Professional cameras typically support 30fps or higher, high-end cameras can reach 60fps or more. A stable frame rate is key to smooth video.

How can I tell if my camera quality is poor?

If you notice: 1) Blurry video; 2) Low frame rate causing stuttering; 3) Color distortion; 4) Poor performance in low light, these may indicate camera quality issues.

What types of cameras are supported?

All types of cameras are supported: laptop built-in cameras, USB external cameras, webcams, conference cameras, live streaming cameras, etc. Compatible with Windows, macOS, and Linux.

How do I test camera resolution?

The tool automatically displays the current resolution. High-quality cameras usually support 1080p (1920x1080) or higher, while standard cameras may only support 720p (1280x720).

Is my test data saved?

All test data is stored only in your current browser session. It will be lost when you refresh the page or close the tab. We do not collect or upload any camera data, ensuring your privacy.
