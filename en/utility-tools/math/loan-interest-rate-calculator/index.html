How is annual interest rate calculated?

Annual rate = (total interest / principal) / loan years √ó 100%. Example: $100k loan, 20 years, $40k interest ‚Üí rate = (40k/100k)/20√ó100% = 2%. Use this calculator to verify.

Equal payment vs decreasing principal?

Equal payment: fixed monthly, front-loaded interest. Decreasing: lower total interest, higher initial payments. The trend chart visualizes the difference.

Term deposit interest same as loan interest?

Term deposit typically uses simple interest: interest = principal √ó rate √ó years. Loans often use compound (equal payment) or simple (interest-only). This calculator helps understand both concepts.
