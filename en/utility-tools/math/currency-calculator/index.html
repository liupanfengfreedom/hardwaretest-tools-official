Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How frequently is the exchange rate data updated?

The exchange rate data used by this tool is updated daily at 16:00 Central European Time (CET), which is 23:00 Beijing Time (Winter) or 22:00 (Summer). After data updates, the tool automatically retrieves the latest exchange rate information.

Why use European Central Bank data?

The European Central Bank is the central bank of the Eurozone, and its published exchange rate data is official and authoritative. The ECB publishes daily EUR exchange rates against other currencies, widely used by global financial institutions. Compared to commercial data sources, ECB data is more stable and reliable.

How are missing data points handled in historical trend charts?

Due to weekends, holidays, or data source limitations, some dates may lack exchange rate records. The chart uses linear interpolation to fill missing data points to ensure chart continuity. However, please note these interpolated points are for reference only, and actual transactions may occur at other times.

Can this tool be used for business decisions?

The information provided by this tool is for reference only and should not be the sole basis for business decisions. Exchange rates are influenced by multiple factors including economic data, political events, and market sentiment. For important financial decisions, consult professional financial advisors and reference multiple data sources.

How do I get historical data for specific currency pairs?

After selecting your currency pair of interest, the tool automatically loads historical data for that pair. Use the time selector to switch between different time ranges (1Y, 5Y, 10Y, 20Y, 30Y) to view historical trends for various periods.

Yes, the Global Exchange Rate Trend Analyzer is completely free to use online, with no registration or login required. We are committed to providing high-quality financial tools to help you better understand the global currency market.
