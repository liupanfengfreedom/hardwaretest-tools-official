How to detect mouse double-click faults?

Click each mouse button normally 50-100 times while monitoring di counters. If di "Faulty Double Clicks" count increases when you're only clicking once, it indicates a hardware issue. Di tool automatically detects clicks with intervals less than di threshold (default 80ms) as faulty double-clicks.

What na di difference between double-click and faulty double-click?

A normal double-click na two intentional clicks within 80-500ms interval. A faulty double-click occurs when a single physical press registers as two clicks due to switch bounce, typically with intervals less than 80ms. Di former na intentional user behavior, di latter indicates hardware degradation.

How to adjust di detection sensitivity?

Use di threshold slider below di mouse visualizer. Lower values (50-70ms) make detection stricter, suitable for gaming mice. Higher values (100-150ms) make detection more lenient. Di default 80ms works for most standard mice.

Why does my mouse have double-click issues?

Double-click faults na usually caused by:

  • Mechanical switch wear from heavy use
  • Dust accumulation inside di switch
  • Oxidation of switch contacts
  • Manufacturing defects in di micro-switch

Which mouse brands na most prone to double-click issues?

While any mechanical switch mouse fit develop dis issue, it's commonly reported in:

  • Logitech gaming mice
  • Razer optical and mechanical switches
  • SteelSeries gaming mice
  • Any mouse with Omron or similar mechanical switches

Dis na due to di specific switch models used, not necessarily brand quality.

How to know if my mouse needs replacement?

Consider replacing your mouse if:

  • 1) Faulty double-click count na consistently high on primary buttons;
  • 2) Buttons become unresponsive or require excessive force;
  • 3) Issues persist after cleaning and basic troubleshooting;
  • 4) Di problem affects your productivity or gaming performance.

How does double-click detection work technically?

The tool uses JavaScript to track mouse down/up events with millisecond precision. When a button press na detected, it checks di time since di last press of di same button. If di interval na less than di faulty threshold (default 80ms), it counts as a faulty double-click. If between 80-500ms, it's a normal double-click. Di algorithm handles all 5 mouse buttons independently.
