Question people dey ask well well (FAQ)

Wetin be wheel backscroll (scroll jitter)?

Wheel backscroll na when you scroll upward and the mouse suddenly jump downward, or you scroll downward and e jump upward. This one usually happen because encoder don gather dust, wear don show, or hardware don begin fail. During test, if scroll goes the opposite direction from the selected mode, the page shows red "!" mark and adds to the reverse error count.

How you fit know say mouse wheel get physical wahala?

Do these checks to know wheel condition:

  • Choose "Up scroll check" mode, then scroll up 20 to 30 times without stopping
  • Check whether red "!" warning go show
  • Watch whether "Reverse Faults" counter dey increase
  • Repeat the same check with downward scroll mode

If reverse faults dey happen often, the wheel likely get physical wahala.

Why average gap time matter for this test?

Average gap time helps show wheel response speed and stability:

  • Below 50ms:very sharp wheel response, like gaming mouse level
  • 50-150ms:normal range for regular office mice
  • 150-300ms:slower wheel response
  • 300ms+:possible wheel wahala or very slow user scroll

Average gap time suppose remain fairly steady during scrolling. If e dey jump too much, that fit point to wahala.

Which mice dey likely get wheel wahala?

Wheel wahala commonly show for these cases:

  • Traditional mice wey still use mechanical encoder
  • Gaming mice wey don see heavy use for long time
  • Any mouse wey dey live for dusty environment

How person fit fix wheel jumping wahala?

Depending on how bad the wahala be, try these repair methods:

  • Small wahala:use compressed air to clean wheel gaps
  • Middle wahala:open the mouse and clean the encoder
  • Serious wahala:replace the wheel encoder part
  • Software wahala:update or reinstall mouse driver

Most physical wahala go need mouse opening for cleaning or part replacement.

How I go know say my mouse wheel need replacement?

Think about repair or replacement when:

  • 1) reverse error count stays high throughout testing;
  • 2) wheel feels sticky or stuck during scroll;
  • 3) wahala still remain after cleaning and basic check;
  • 4) wheel no dey answer at all or you need too much force;
  • 5) the wahala don begin affect your work or gaming.

Dis page helps you make better decision because the result shows the wahala in clear numbers.

How wheel checking take work for deep side?

The page uses JavaScript to monitor wheel events and work out these values:

  • Scroll direction:determined from the deltaY value, whether up or down
  • Scroll interval:calculated from the time gap between scroll events
  • Reverse detection:compares the real scroll direction with the selected test mode
  • Scroll precision:measures how many lines each scroll step move, often 3 to 5 lines

All calculations happen locally for your page app live, so no server work dey needed.

Wireless mouse fit use this page?

Yes, this page works with both wired and wireless mice. Just make sure your wireless mouse connect well and battery still strong. Some wireless mice fit get slightly different wheel behavior, but the check principle stays the same. If the outcome looks strange, try these things:

  • replace the battery with fresh one
  • pair the receiver again
  • test in wired mode if the mouse supports am
  • try another USB port
