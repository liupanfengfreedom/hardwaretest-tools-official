Question wey people dey ask well well (FAQ)

What na CPS?

CPS (Clicks Per Second) na di number of mouse clicks per second, an important metric for measuring mouse click speed. It reflects your finger reaction speed and click frequency. Average users typically have CPS between 4-8, while gamers fit achieve 10-15 or more.

How to improve CPS score?

Improving CPS requires practice and technique: 1) Maintain relaxed wrist posture; 2) Use correct finger muscles; 3) Practice rhythm; 4) Use suitable mouse; 5) Regular training to maintain muscle memory. Recommended to start with 5-second mode and gradually increase difficulty.

Why do test results fluctuate?

CPS test results may fluctuate due to various factors: 1) Finger fatigue level; 2) Ambient temperature; 3) Mouse surface material; 4) Mental state; 5) Test time. Recommended to take multiple tests under same conditions and average di results.

What na di differences between test modes?

Different test modes target different abilities:

  • 1-second burst test: Test maximum instantaneous click speed
  • 5-second precision test: Test short-term stable clicking ability
  • 10-second standard test: Comprehensive click ability assessment
  • 60-second endurance test: Test long-term sustained clicking ability

Why test different mouse buttons?

Testing different buttons comprehensively assesses mouse operation ability:

  • Left-click test: Standard clicking ability, applicable to most applications
  • Right-click test: Pinky flexibility, important for certain games
  • Middle-click test: Middle finger strength and precision
  • Any mode: Overall coordination ability

How to evaluate my CPS level?

Based on statistical data, CPS levels na roughly:

  • Average: 4-8 CPS
  • Good: 8-12 CPS
  • Excellent: 12-16 CPS
  • Pro: 16+ CPS

Note: Results may vary between different test modes.

What na practical applications of CPS testing?

CPS testing has practical applications in multiple fields:

  • Gaming performance: Affects reaction speed in FPS games
  • Office productivity: Affects data entry and document processing speed
  • Hand coordination: Serves as assessment metric for hand coordination
  • Rehabilitation training: Used for recovery assessment after hand injuries
